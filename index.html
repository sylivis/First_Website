I hope you like the background. It was a picture taken in some of the southern-most mountains of New Mexico, right outside of Alamagordo. I was actually sitting here a month ago when I decided to take this course. I was staying with family, as my father had just passed. We gathered for the funeral the morning in which this picture had been taken. The decision I made to attend this boot camp stems from a desire to move back to that small town and be surrounded again by family. I hope to work remotely there once I have graduated, provided that I find work.

I had learned some coding off and on over the past few years. I had even taken Computer Science 101 when I was still in college. I know the basics of JavaScript, some Krotlin, and now, after completing the prework, some Python, though nowhere near enough of any of it. The logic I have come across while coding always makes sense, and I have yet to come across a problem that I believe I would be unable to solve eventually More importantly, the more I learn about development, the more I want to know. I have yet to find any practical skill I enjoy more than programing. I look forward to learning what I can while in this course.

Feel free to browse the website. I assume you are to grade its merit afterall. If the mood suits you, you can even buy me a cup of coffee. This boot camp was expensive after all!
